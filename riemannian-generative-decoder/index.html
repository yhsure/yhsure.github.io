tl;dr: Simpler representation learning on manifolds. We propose a decoder-only framework to learn latents on arbitrary Riemannian manifolds via maximum likelihood and Riemannian optimization. We highlight its use with biological case studies.

Abstract

Riemannian representation learning typically relies on complex density approximations on specific manifolds. We introduce the Riemannian generative decoder, which discards the encoder and jointly optimizes manifold-valued latents via Riemannian Adam alongside a decoder network. This encoder-free scheme handles any Riemannian manifold, simplifies constraints, and uses geometry-aware regularization through input noise. We validate on three case studies—a branching diffusion process, human mitochondrial DNA, and cell-division cycle data—demonstrating geometry-aligned, interpretable latent spaces.
